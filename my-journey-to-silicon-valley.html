My journey to Silicon Valley

— 02/02/2016 —

About 9 months ago I discovered Paul Graham’s essays.

This was a live changing moment for me!

He has ignited the passion for startups in me. Since then I have been following Y-Combinator, the startup accelerator he co-founded, closely. It became my dream to join the Y-Combinator family one day.

I am aware of the fact that there are several top accelerator programs out there which are doing a great job as well.

Pillars of my strategy for the next two years

  • If you want to build a great company, you need a great product.
  • If you want to have a great product, you have to iterate a lot.
  • If you want to iterate a lot, you need quick iteration cycles.

  • If you want to have quick iteration cycles, you need to be able to write good code really fast.
    Speed wins!

    In the next two years I will focus mainly on my software development skills.

Concrete steps

  • Become a really good Full Stack Developer
  • Finish my studies in Electrical Engineering
  • Move to Silicon Valley and work for a tech giant for a few years to build a network
  • Start a company
  • Get into YC

Right now, I am in Chiang Mai (Thailand) to work on my goals without distraction.

What am I doing in Chiang Mai?

I basically work on three things:

  1. Blog
    Write some articles for my blog to improve my communication and language skills. My literature teacher won’t believe it, but I actually do enjoy writing now ;-)

  2. Programming skills
    Going through some tutorials and write some code for a side project.

  3. Product skills
    I work on a side project called “HabitControl”. I use it to improve my product skills. I basically interview random people here and ask for feedback. After some tests, I will experiment with some launching strategies on hackernews. Just to get a basic understanding of what works and what doensn’t.

I will update this post from time to time.

— 05/20/2016 —

I am back in Germany now continuing my studies.
